# Business Analysis

Business Analysis is the practice of enabling change by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders.

---

## Role of Business Analysis

Business analysis activities include:
- Understanding current state (as-is)
- Defining future state (to-be)
- Identifying gaps and opportunities
- Eliciting and documenting requirements
- Evaluating solution options
- Supporting implementation and change

---

## Key Techniques

| Technique | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Stakeholder analysis** | Identify who to engage |
| **Process mapping** | Document current and future processes |
| **Requirements workshops** | Elicit needs collaboratively |
| **Use cases/User stories** | Capture functional requirements |
| **Gap analysis** | Identify differences between states |
| **MoSCoW prioritisation** | Prioritise requirements |
{:class="table table-single table-narrow"}

---

## Analysis Outputs

| Output | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Current state analysis** | Documentation of as-is situation |
| **Requirements specification** | Documented business and system needs |
| **Process models** | Visual representation of workflows |
| **Options analysis** | Evaluation of solution alternatives |
| **Impact assessment** | Analysis of change implications |
{:class="table table-single table-narrow"}

## Best Practices

- Engage stakeholders early and often
- Use visual models to communicate
- Validate understanding continuously
- Maintain traceability
- Consider the whole system
